Get MOE Number

Simplified Process

The MOE (Ministry of Environment) in Ontario regulates the transportation, handling, and disposal of hazardous waste, hazardous materials, and other types of potentially harmful substances. This includes, but is not limited to, the following types of cargo:

  • Chemicals, including those used in manufacturing and industrial processes
  • Petroleum products, including gasoline, diesel fuel, and oil
  • Medical waste, including sharps, infectious waste, and pharmaceuticals
  • Radioactive materials, including those used in medical imaging and nuclear power generation
  • Asbestos and other hazardous building materials
  • Pesticides and other agricultural chemicals
  • Industrial solvents and cleaners

If your business produces, handles, transports, or disposes of any of these types of materials in Ontario, you may be required to obtain a MOE number and comply with all applicable regulations. It's important to consult with a qualified expert to determine your specific requirements and ensure that you're in compliance with all relevant laws and regulations.

If you're looking to obtain a MOE (Ministry of Environment) number in Ontario, one option you may consider is the EASR (Environmental Activity and Sector Registry) program.

The EASR program is designed to streamline the process of obtaining MOE numbers for low-risk environmental activities, such as small-scale waste management, air and noise emissions, and water-taking. This program is intended to be a more efficient and cost-effective alternative to traditional Environmental Compliance Approvals (ECAs).

By obtaining a MOE number through the EASR program, you'll be able to demonstrate your compliance with all applicable environmental regulations and operate your business with peace of mind. This program is particularly well-suited for small and medium-sized businesses that don't require the same level of scrutiny as larger operations.
