Upgrade Your Business with IRP/IFTA

Everything you need to know when getting those for the first time!

Do you need to travel outside of your province or state? Do you operate a heavy truck 26,000lbs or more? If so, it's time to simplify the complexities of license plates and other stickers.

What is IRP? and why do I need one?

Let's make things super simple, and consider the following example.

Imagine you have only one truck, and that truck travels between Ontario and Quebec. This truck accumulated equal mileage in both provinces, say 100 clicks each.

Now let's say you paid $2000 in license plate fees to Ontario. And you paid zero to Quebec. Quebec gets upset and it wants its piece of the pie. In this particular case a fair split would be 50/50. Since the distance traveled is the same.

So simply put IRP (International Registration Plan) splits the license plate fees 'pie' between all provinces and states you travel to.

What about IFTA?

Well, along the route you had to fuel as well. Say you fuel in Ontario all the time. The Quebec tax man is upset. They are missing fuel tax dollars.

IFTA (International Fuel Tax Agreement) re-distributes fuel tax based on distance traveled and fuel bought.

And it may sound like they are trying to make your life really complicated, but actually it's the opposite.

I've been doing these permits since the program was introduced in 2001. The alternative is purchasing single trip permits, everytime you cross the border of a province or state. Some states used to have their own license plates that you had to purchase and display. It was a mess.

The process of applying for IRP/IFTA for the first time may seem daunting and unusual. They really don't like over the counter service. And they push you towards doing everything remote. They even push for couriers to deliver your plates. So I'm here to guide you every step of the way. By getting IRP/IFTA for the first time, you'll be able to focus on what you do best - running your business and serving your customers. You'll save time and money, reduce administrative costs, and ensure compliance with all applicable regulations.
