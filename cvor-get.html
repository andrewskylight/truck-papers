CVOR Ontario

CVOR (Commercial Vehicle Operator's Registration) - an Ontario permit.

Much like you have a permit that shows you can drive a vehicle, a business can get a permit to buy (plate and operate)a commercial vehicle.

Now, while everyone in Ontario needs to have a driver's license to drive a vehicle, the businesses are required to get a CVOR license, when:

  • Trucks: gross weight is 4,500kg or more (includes trailer and everything that pushes onto the ground)
  • Busses: seating capacity is ten or more
  • Tow-trucks: all without exception

There there are a number of exceptions to the rule, and a few steps to the process, so we had dedicated a web-site to getting your CVOR certificate:

cvortest.com

For all intents and purposes CVOR is a monitoring system. The CVOR permit is a privilege. While anybody who passes a road-test is going to be issued a license, not everyone is going to be issued a CVOR after passing a CVOR test. It may get people by surprise, but it happens.

I had successfully appealed a number of "Notices of Refusal to Issue CVOR Certificate".

You can find more details here:

Appeal Notice of Refusal

A CVOR system has a 'demerit point' system. It is more complicated.

In addition to having points assigned, the CVOR license is going to have a worded rating assigned.

Most folks will start with "SATISFACTORY-UNAUDITED".

In the event of a failed facility audit, the rating gets downgraded to "CONDITIONAL".
